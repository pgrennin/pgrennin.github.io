Exploring My Values

Sunday, September 21, 2014
In this blog I've been tasked with exploring my values. Here are some of the values that I think are important to me.

  • Accomplishment
  • Achievement
  • Efficiency
  • Health
  • Honesty
  • Personal development
  • Self-reliance
  • Wealth
  • Industriousness
  • Persistence
One value that I think is especially important to me is efficiency. I'm always looking for ways to improve how I spend my time and money. I also spend a lot of time thinking of ways to complete tasks and goals more efficiently. I think this is hard-wired into my personality ( I'm an ISTJ according to the Myers Brigg Type Indicator). I enjoy reading books on productivity and self improvement. I also dive into books on personal finance and investing. It all ties with my desire for efficiency. The downside of this is sometimes I can come across as uncaring and detached.

In general, I try to live up to all these values as much as I can. I feel like I have a natural attraction toward them as a result of my personality, life experience, and environment I grew up in. One thing that this exercise has reminded me of is that everybody is different and has a different set of value as a result of their personality and life experience.

One topic that I sometimes get asked for advice on is personal finance and investing. I'm a very rational decision maker and I have experience successfully investing in stocks, funds, and real estate. I think this ties in with my natural strengths such as efficiency. I have an economics degree and have read several books on investing which allows me to hold my own in any conversation on the subject.

There are several things I do to help when I feel down or stressed. If I'm worrying about a problem I try to break down the worry by analyzing what's the worst that can happen, then I try to improve on the worse, a technique I learned from Dale Carnegie's book. I also find meditation and prayer a great way to reduce my stress.
